Tripura: A Treasure of Natural Beauty and Rich Culture

Unakoti Archaeological Site

Unakoti, famously known as the 'Angkor Wat of the North-East', is a sculptural emblem and ancient Shaivite place hosting rock carvings, figures, and images of gods and goddesses. The central Shiva head, known as Unakotiswara Kal Bhairava, is about 30 feet high, including an embroidered head-dress. It is vying for a UNESCO world heritage tag and is a major Hindu Shaivite pilgrimage site[4).

Neermahal Water Palace

Located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, Neermahal Palace is one of the two water palaces in India. It features a royal Antar Mahal, guest rooms, and a courtyard, offering a stunning view of its surroundings.

Tripura Sundari Temple

Built by Maharaja Dhanya Manikya in 1501 AD, the Tripura Sundari Temple is one of the 51 Peethas and is dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari, an avatar of Goddess Parvati. The temple is situated on a hill and features a lake with tortoises.

Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

This sanctuary is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including capped langur, spectacled monkey, clouded leopard, and barking deer. It also features coffee and rubber plantations and offers boat rides and toy train rides.

Jampui Hills

Located in the Northern part of Tripura, Jampui Hills offer scenic views, beautiful valleys, and are known for their juicy oranges. The best time to visit is in November, and the area is also home to a variety of wildlife species.

Ujjayanta Palace

Reconstructed in 1901 AD by Maharaja Radha Kishore Manikya, Ujjayanta Palace features a gorgeous Mughal garden with musical fountains, velvety grasslands, and artificial lakes. It is now home to the Tripura State Museum.

Bhubaneswari Temple

Located near the Ujjayanta Palace, the Bhubaneswari Temple is another significant religious site in Tripura, dedicated to Goddess Bhubaneswari.
